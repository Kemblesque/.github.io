Visual Hierarchy

Global Cutlery

globalcutleryusa.com

There are several principles for visual hierarchy in relationship to web design. This image best shows the principle known as Z-patterns. Z-patterns display information in a pattern that the readers eyes are most likely to use, first scanning the top of the page where important information is located. Second follows the reader down to the oppisite corner in the lower portion of the screen and then follows across the bottom, creating a z pattern.

Hicks Law

Amazon

amazon.com

Hicks Law states that the more choices someone has the longer it will take them to make a decision. Amazon has perfected this with the option to buy an item with 1 click. Allowing someone to add an item to a cart or buy it right then displays hicks law by only giving two options to purchase, and one of them is easier than the other, making impules purchases happen.
